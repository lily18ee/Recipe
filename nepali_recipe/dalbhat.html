DAL BHAT


Ingredients

**For Dal (Lentil Soup):**

  • 1 cup red or yellow lentils (masoor or toor dal)
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, minced
  • 1-2 green chilies, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil or ghee
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

**For Bhat (Rice):**

  • 1 cup rice
  • 2 cups water
  • A pinch of salt

Instructions

**For Dal (Lentil soup):**

  1. Rinse the lentils thoroughly and set them aside.
  2. In a large saucepan, heat the oil or ghee over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and allow them to sizzle.
  3. Add chopped onions and sauté until they turn translucent.
  4. Stir in the minced garlic, ginger, and green chilies. Sauté for a few minutes until the raw smell disappears.
  5. Add the rinsed lentils and stir for a couple of minutes.
  6. Pour in the water, add turmeric powder, and salt. Stir well.
  7. Cover the saucepan and let the dal simmer for about 20-25 minutes, or until the lentils are soft and fully cooked. You may need to add more water if it becomes too thick.
  8. Garnish with fresh cilantro.

**For Bhat (Rice):**

  1. Rinse the rice until the water runs clear.
  2. In a large pot, add rice, water, and a pinch of salt. Stir.
  3. Bring it to a boil, then reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for 15-20 minutes, or until the rice is tender and the water is absorbed.

**To Serve Dal Bhat:**

A portion of cooked rice (Bhat) on a plate, alongside the lentil soup (Dal). You can enjoy it with various accompaniments like pickles, vegetables, and sometimes, meat dishes. It's a staple and nutritious meal in Nepal. Enjoy your homemade Nepali Dal Bhat!
